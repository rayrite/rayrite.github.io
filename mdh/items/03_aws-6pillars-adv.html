AWS Well-Architected Framework: Condensed Study Guide (C.O.R.P.S.S.)

1. Cost Optimization Pillar

Core Purpose: The ability to run systems to deliver business value at the lowest price point.

Design Principles:
  • Implement Cloud Financial Management: Have a dedicated practice for managing and optimizing cloud costs.
  • Adopt a consumption model: Pay only for what you use; shut down unused resources.
  • Measure overall efficiency: Monitor costs and usage to understand ROI.
  • Stop spending money on data center operations: Let AWS handle the heavy lifting.
  • Analyze and attribute expenditure: Use tags and Cost Explorer to get a clear view of AWS spend.

2. Operational Excellence Pillar

Core Purpose: The ability to run and monitor systems to deliver business value and to continuously improve supporting processes and procedures.

Design Principles:
  • Perform operations as code: Automate deployments, infrastructure changes, and responses to events. (e.g., CloudFormation).
  • Make frequent, small, reversible changes: Reduces blast radius of failures and makes rollbacks easier.
  • Refine operations procedures frequently: Evolve procedures and learn from operational events.
  • Anticipate failure: Proactively identify and plan for potential failures. Run "Game Days" to test responses.
  • Learn from all operational failures: Treat every failure as a learning opportunity.

3. Reliability Pillar

Core Purpose: The ability of a workload to perform its intended function correctly and consistently and to recover quickly from failures.

Design Principles:
  • Automatically recover from failure: Design systems to detect failures and auto-heal. (e.g., ELB health checks, Auto Scaling).
  • Test recovery procedures: Regularly test recovery plans by simulating failures (e.g., Chaos engineering).
  • Scale horizontally to increase aggregate workload availability: Replace one large server with multiple small ones.
  • Stop guessing capacity: Monitor demand and automatically scale resources to meet it. (e.g., Auto Scaling).
  • Manage change in automation: Use automation to manage all infrastructure changes to reduce human error.

4. Performance Efficiency Pillar

Core Purpose: The ability to use computing resources efficiently to meet system requirements and maintain that efficiency as demand changes.

Design Principles:
  • Democratize advanced technologies: Use managed services to avoid needing deep expertise.
  • Go global in minutes: Deploy in multiple AWS Regions to reduce latency.
  • Use serverless architectures: Eliminate operational overhead of managing servers. (e.g., Lambda, Fargate).
  • Experiment more often: Use the low cost of cloud to test and find the most performant configurations.
  • Consider mechanical sympathy: Choose the technology that aligns best with your goals.

5. Security Pillar

Core Purpose: The ability to protect information, systems, and assets while delivering business value through risk assessments and mitigation strategies.

Design Principles:
  • Implement a strong identity foundation: Centralize identity management and enforce least privilege. (e.g., IAM).
  • Enable traceability: Monitor, alert, and audit all actions and changes in real time. (e.g., CloudTrail, CloudWatch).
  • Apply security at all layers: Apply security to network, instances, applications, and data. (e.g., NACLs, Security Groups, WAF).
  • Automate security best practices: Use automation to apply security controls at scale.
  • Protect data in transit and at rest: Encrypt all sensitive data everywhere. (e.g., ACM, KMS).
  • Keep people away from data: Reduce or eliminate direct human access to sensitive data.

6. Sustainability Pillar

Core Purpose: The ability to continuously improve sustainability impacts by reducing energy consumption and increasing efficiency.

Design Principles:
  • Understand your impact: Measure the environmental impact of your cloud workload.
  • Establish sustainability goals: Set goals for reducing resources required per transaction.
  • Maximize utilization: Choose the smallest number and size of resources that meet business needs.
  • Anticipate and adopt new, more efficient hardware and software offerings: Plan to adopt power-efficient options like Graviton.
  • Use managed services: Transfer responsibility for hardware optimization to AWS for greater efficiency.

The Six Advantages of Cloud Computing

Advantage Description
1. Trade capital expense for variable expense Instead of investing heavily in data centers and servers before you know how you’re going to use them, you can pay only when you consume computing resources, and pay only for how much you consume.
2. Benefit from massive economies of scale By using cloud computing, you can achieve a lower variable cost than you can get on your own. Because usage from hundreds of thousands of customers is aggregated in the cloud, providers such as AWS can achieve higher economies of scale, which translates into lower pay as-you-go prices.
3. Stop guessing capacity Eliminate guessing on your infrastructure capacity needs. When you make a capacity decision prior to deploying an application, you often end up either sitting on expensive idle resources or dealing with limited capacity. With cloud computing, these problems go away. You can access as much or as little capacity as you need, and scale up and down as required with only a few minutes’ notice.
4. Increase speed and agility In a cloud computing environment, new IT resources are only a click away, which means that you reduce the time it takes to make those resources available to your developers from weeks to just minutes. This results in a dramatic increase in agility for the organization, since the cost and time it takes to experiment and develop is significantly lower.
5. Stop spending money running and maintaining data centers Focus on projects that differentiate your business, not the infrastructure. Cloud computing lets you focus on your own customers, rather than on the heavy lifting of racking, stacking, and powering servers.
6. Go global in minutes Easily deploy your application in multiple regions around the world with just a few clicks. This means you can provide lower latency and a better experience for your customers at minimal cost.
